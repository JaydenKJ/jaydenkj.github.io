Treasures of the Shore

Upon the sandy shoreline, where the ocean meets the land,
Lie treasures of the deep blue, shaped by nature's hand.
Shells of every color, from ivory white to pink,
Hold secrets of the sea within their spiral links. Each shell tells a story, a whisper from the waves,
Of ocean currents wandering through ancient underwater caves.
From tiny, speckled cowries to the conch’s hollowed sound,
They carry echoes of the sea, where mysteries abound. Smooth and polished by the tide, each shell is a little gem,
A snapshot of the ocean’s heart, a treasure at the rim.
Collected by the beachcomber, or left for tides to claim,
These sea-worn fragments speak to us, and call us by name. So gather up these wonders, and let their beauty show,
The ocean’s endless artistry in each soft ebb and flow.
For in their fragile patterns, a timeless truth is told—
The sea’s great gift of wonder, both precious and bold.
