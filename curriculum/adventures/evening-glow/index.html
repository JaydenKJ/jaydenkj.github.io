Evening Glow

He climbs the mountain, stone and stride,
With heartbeats loud and breath as guide,
Through crags and cliffs, through trails unknown,
To heights where whispered winds are blown. The sun is sinking, low and gold,
The peak is near, the sky unfolds,
His muscles ache, his spirit soars,
With every step, the summit roars. At last he stands where earth meets sky,
A silent witness to the high,
He turns to gaze, his heart aglow,
Into the valley’s twilight show. A canvas spread in hues profound,
Where day’s last light and shadows blend,
The valley lies in amber tones,
A realm of peace where silence moans. Mountains cradle valleys deep,
In shades of dusk where secrets sleep,
The rivers gleam like liquid fire,
A tranquil sea of light and desire. He breathes the chill of twilight’s breath,
In awe of beauty’s subtle depth,
The world unfolds, serene and vast,
A fleeting moment, bound to last.
